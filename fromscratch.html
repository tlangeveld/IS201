The Battle of Length

As you can probably tell, I am pretty passionate about my fantasy books. I am always up for a good discussion (debate) about what books are best and which ones are overrated. Recently, I had a chancce to prove my own opinions right as well as use some VBA skills. I am taking a Young Adult Literature class, and my professor has been pretty vocal about his disdain for fantasy, citing the length of the books as his main reason why. Of course, as a fan of the genre, I could not let that slide. For a research paper in the class, I gathered data about the average user enjoyment of books and how that enjoyment related to their length. My hypothesis was that due to the higher complexity and depth of longer books, they would be more enjoyable to the people who read them.

The Gathering

To get data, I went through a list of the top selling books of all time, finding their word counts and then finding their GoodReads rating. After finding 15 books in each length group, which I classified as "Short" (<100,000 words), "Medium" (100,000-150,000 words), and "Long" (>150,000 words), I wrote a nice little VBA program that found the average GoodReads rating and word count for each length group. My results proved my hypothesis correct! I was able to provide numerical evidence (albeit from a small sample size) that the average reader likes long books better!
